## Standard Whale Watching
The standard whale watching trip is up to 4 hours and departs from Moss Landing. It is designed for whale, dolphin, and marine wildlife encounters on Monterey Bay.

Additional website details:
- Whale-watching product page describes 3.5 to 4 hour trips and lists variants including 2-3 hour and 3-4 hour options.
- Capacity is set by departure.
- Ages are listed as all ages.
- The page states free cancellation up to 48 hours before departure.

## Sunset Whale Watching
The sunset whale watching cruise is up to 3 hours and is a more relaxed evening wildlife trip.

## All-Day Trip
The all-day trip is up to 9 hours and is for guests who want a longer Monterey Bay wildlife experience.

## Photography Tour
The marine wildlife photography tour is designed for guests who want more time and flexibility for photography.

Note: another related product appears on site as Wildlife Cruise and Photography Workshop (up to 7 hr 30 min).

## Private Charters
Private charters are available subject to availability. The agent should collect the desired date, group size, occasion, and contact information, then escalate to the crew.

## Vessel
The main vessel, Sanctuary, is a 43-foot Delta Marine twin-engine whale watching vessel. It has indoor seating, outside seating, a heated cabin, and a marine head.

Additional vessel details from the vessel page:
- Coast Guard certified and fully insured
- Indoor cabin with upholstered seating
- Covered and uncovered outdoor deck areas
- Twin Cummins diesels
- Operated with guest count set by departure conditions

## Wildlife
Whales, dolphins, sea lions, sea otters, seabirds, and other marine wildlife may be seen on Monterey Bay. Wildlife sightings are common, but specific animals cannot be guaranteed.

FAQ page includes direct questions about guarantees, weather, rough conditions, accessibility, and dogs/service-animal-adjacent cases. These should be handled conservatively and escalated when policy-sensitive.

Marine mammal seasonality (source-backed from Sanctuary Cruises marine-life pages):
- Humpback Whale: year round, peak Apr-Nov
- Blue Whale: Jun-Oct
- Fin Whale: year round, peak Sep-Dec
- Gray Whale: Dec-May
- Baird's Beaked Whale: Jun-Oct
- Orca/Killer Whale: year round, peak Apr-May
- Risso's Dolphin: year round
- Pacific White-Sided Dolphin: year round, peak winter
- Long-Beaked Common Dolphin: summer and fall
- Northern Right Whale Dolphin: year round
- Pacific Offshore Bottlenose Dolphin: year round (unpredictable)
- Sea Otter: year round
- California Sea Lion: year round
- Harbor Seal: year round
- Steller Sea Lion: fall-spring
- Northern Elephant Seal: winter-spring

## Booking System
Bookings are completed through FareHarbor. The agent may help guests choose a trip and find demo availability, but final payment and confirmation must happen through FareHarbor or with a human crew member.

Booking links on the site route through FareHarbor and show live inventory/pricing.

Calendar and scheduling data files in this repo:
- `data/whale_watch_schedule_next_3_months.json`: scraped snapshot of whale-watch date/time options for June-August 2026
- `data/marine_mammals_reference.json`: species and seasonality reference data scraped from Sanctuary Cruises marine-life pages

## Escalation Rules
Escalate to a human for refunds, cancellations, rescheduling, weather decisions, seasickness or medical concerns, service animals, accessibility accommodations, private charter details, payment problems, complaints, or anything urgent within 24 hours of departure.
