# Sanctuary Cruises — Agent Knowledge Base

## Company Overview

**Sanctuary Cruises** is a whale watching and marine wildlife tour company based at Moss Landing harbor on Monterey Bay, California. It is owned and operated by captain Michael Sack and is widely regarded as offering among the best whale watching access on the Pacific Coast.

- **Address**: 7881 Sandholdt Road, Moss Landing, CA 95039
- **Phone**: 831-917-1042
- **Email**: crew@sanctuarycruises.com
- **Website**: sanctuarycruises.com
- **FareHarbor booking page**: fareharbor.com/embeds/book/sanctuarycruises

Moss Landing is located at the head of the Monterey Submarine Canyon, one of the deepest ocean canyons on the Pacific Coast. The canyon funnels cold, nutrient-rich water to the surface year-round, sustaining some of the highest concentrations of marine mammals in the world.

## Wildlife — What to Expect

### Signature Species

**Humpback Whales** (*Megaptera novaeangliae*)
- Peak season: April to December, best May to November
- Known for breaching, lunge-feeding, and pec-slapping
- Frequently spotted near the canyon edge

**Blue Whales** (*Balaenoptera musculus*)
- Peak season: July to October
- World's largest animal; typically visible in mid-to-late summer offshore
- Long, slow surfacings — often 3–5 blows visible per surfacing cycle

**Gray Whales** (*Eschrichtius robustus*)
- Peak season: December to April (southbound) and March to May (northbound)
- Migrate past Monterey Bay; mothers with calves sometimes rest close to shore

**Orca / Killer Whales** (*Orcinus orca*)
- Seen year-round, most active February to June
- Transient orcas pass through hunting marine mammals; offshore ecotypes hunt squid

**Risso's Dolphins** (*Grampus griseus*)
- Year-round, especially spring and fall
- Heavily scarred adults are distinctive; often found in large pods

**Pacific White-Sided Dolphins** (*Lagenorhynchus obliquidens*)
- Year-round in offshore waters
- Highly acrobatic; often seen in large super-pods

**Common Dolphins** (*Delphinus delphis*)
- Year-round; often associated with feeding frenzies
- Very fast swimmers; frequently bowride

**Northern Right Whale Dolphins** (*Lissodelphis borealis*)
- Offshore, typically fall and winter
- Distinctive — no dorsal fin, dark coloring

### Pinnipeds and Marine Mammals

**California Sea Lions** (*Zalophus californianus*)
- Year-round; common near harbor and on buoys
- Often seen surfing bow waves

**Steller Sea Lions** (*Eumetopias jubatus*)
- Winter and spring
- Much larger than California sea lions; endangered subspecies (Western DPS)

**Northern Fur Seals** (*Callorhinus ursinus*)
- Offshore, fall and winter

**Harbor Seals** (*Phoca vitulina*)
- Year-round; usually resting in small groups on rocks or floating kelp

**Southern Sea Otters** (*Enhydra lutris nereis*)
- Year-round in Monterey Bay, especially kelp beds near Moss Landing
- Threatened species; held protected status since 1977

**Harbor Porpoise** (*Phocoena phocoena*)
- Year-round near shore; shy, small, and often overlooked

### Basking Sharks and Ocean Sunfish

**Ocean Sunfish / Mola Mola** (*Mola mola*)
- July to October
- Sunbath on the surface; look like giant round pancakes
- Can weigh over 5,000 pounds

**Basking Sharks** (*Cetorhinus maximus*)
- Spring and early summer; open mouth filter-feeding at surface

### Seabirds

Common species include Sooty Shearwaters, Brown Pelicans, Brandt's Cormorants, Common Murres, Rhinoceros Auklets, Pigeon Guillemots, Black-footed Albatross, and Sabine's Gulls. Many are only visible offshore.

## What to Bring

- Warm layers (jacket, hat, gloves — even in summer)
- Sunscreen and sunglasses
- Non-drowsy motion sickness medication if susceptible (take 30–60 min before boarding)
- Snacks and water (light snacks recommended to avoid seasickness)
- Camera or binoculars
- Your FareHarbor confirmation (email or printed)
- Arrive at least 20 minutes before departure

---

## Seasickness Guidance

Monterey Bay has open-ocean swells. Guests prone to motion sickness should:
- Take non-drowsy medication before boarding
- Stay on deck and focus on the horizon
- Avoid heavy meals before the trip
- Try ginger candies or pressure wristbands
- Consult a doctor before the trip if uncertain
